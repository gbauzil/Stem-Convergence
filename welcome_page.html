Why Should You Pursue A Career In STEM...



There are many different possibilities that pursuing a STEM field can provide. STEM careers


give high salaries immediately following college graduation which can be useful when paying off student debt.


There is a growing rate of opportunities in the STEM fields and it will continue to grow as long as


technoogy is continuing to develop. Salaries will increase based off of the degree earned. The career


of your choice can be used to make a positive impact on society. Majors such as computer science, robotics, and engineering


can lead to the creation of innovative products. STEM majors also have more flexibility when it comes to


what you want to do with your STEM degree [1].



Women In STEM...


Despite the benefits STEM careers may provide, there is an unfortunate gap between men and women in these fields.


Fields tht are mostly centered around engineering and technology are male dominated making women less likely to pursue a STEM career


Women also earn less in pay than their male counterparts in STEM fields [2]. Women of color earn even less degrees in


STEM careers along with asian and latinas. "Women in the US represented 25.5% of computer and mathematical occupations"


and "16.2% of architecture and engineering occupations"[2]. There are not many role models for women in STEM compared to men.


Mentors that young women can look up to can create an effective learning environment that can encourage women to pursue


a certain career path [3]. Aside from the lack of role models, there is also a sexist atmosphere within the workplace. This can push


women away from a STEM career. Stereotypes for women are detrimental to the presence of more women in the STEM fields.


There is often doubt on whether women are capable of doing the work required for a STEM job which can create an unwelcoming


environment.
